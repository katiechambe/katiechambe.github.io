Outreach

Warrior scholar project - 2019

In the summer of 2019, I worked with the Warrior Scholar Project as a research project leader during the STEM week hosted at the University of Arizona. I worked with 5 of the 15 students who participated in the WSP Arizona program, and created as series of Jupyter Notebooks. The first notebook provided the students with an introduction programming concepts in python. The following notebooks then built upon these learned programming skills to simulate exoplanet orbits and analyze light curve data from the Kepler Space Telescope database. The students learned about exoplanet dynamics and detection methods, and presented their own exoplanet orbital models at the end of the program.

Warrior scholar project - 2020

I volunteered again with the Warrior Scholar Project in the summer of 2020. I was the head research project leader, and helped manage the research project with 2 other graduate students from the University of Arizona. From my experience with the WSP research projects in 2019, I was able to adapt the Jupyter Notebooks to better suit the needs of the students in the WSP program. We spent a lot of time discussing exoplanet habitability and the search for intelligent life. For some insight into my own lessons learned and takeaways from leading the project this year, including some of the unique challenges that we faced from the pandemic, see Lessons learned from WSP.
