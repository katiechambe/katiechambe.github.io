Bio

Hello! I'm Katie Chamberlain, a 6th year Astronomy and Astrophysics PhD candidate at the University in Arizona. My thesis work focuses on the behavior of galaxy pairs in large volume cosmological simulations, and I will be defending my dissertation in May 2024. I got my Bachelor's degree in Physics and in Mathematics at Montana State University in Bozeman, MT.

Welcome! I am Katie Chamberlain, a 6th year Astronomy PhD candidate at the University of Arizona, specializing in theoretical extragalactic astronomy with a focus on the behavior of galaxy pairs in large volume cosmological simulations. I am passionate about the “open source” part of open source software, and believe that community developed and maintained software is the foundation upon which modern science is enabled. I think of myself as a creative problem solver before I think of myself as an astronomer, because I have a wide range of interests in different disciplines, ranging from astronomy to linguistics or architecture. I am particularly interested in the overlap of art and science -- not space art, not physics-accurate artistic endeavors, but the place where artistic and scientific practice overlap and what can be learned there. In addition to my thesis work as a graduate student, I have worked with the Warrior Scholar Program to teach introductory programming skills to military veterans who are considering pursuing higher education after their service. I developed a set of Jupyter Notebooks to guide students through a one-week intensive research project about exoplanets, and taught the students how to give a research presentation about their findings at the end of the week. The Jupyter Notebooks associated with this program are available to the public on my GitHub page: https://github.com/katiechambe/WSP2020.
